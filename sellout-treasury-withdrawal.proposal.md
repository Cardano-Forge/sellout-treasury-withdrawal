## 1. Abstract

This treasury withdrawal funds Phase 2 of a production Cardano-native ticketing system operated by Sellout.io and built by Anvil Development Agency.

Phase 1 (Completed): On-chain event creation, CIP-68 ticket minting, ticket transfers, and attendance verification. Phase 1 is already live on Cardano mainnet, funded entirely by Sellout (over $130k invested), with no Cardano-related funding, as a tie-in to their existing product.

Phase 2 delivers the components required to scale this system to mass adoption:

- A secondary marketplace with on-chain royalty enforcement
- Anti-scalping controls configurable per event
- Wallet onboarding capable of serving Sellout's 200,000+ existing users
- Organizer-facing tools
- An independent third-party security audit
- A professional marketing-firm launch amplification campaign

Phase 2 is delivered through a new operating entity that consolidates the Phase 1 IP and pays a defined revenue share to the Cardano Treasury permanently (see §4, Funding & Treasury Repayment Structure). We are committing to repay $1,093,231 to the Cardano Treasury through this mechanism.

The anchor deployment is the Yellowstone Club 2026 concert series — a contracted lineup including Noah Kahan, Sting, Weezer, The Black Crows, Mumford & Sons, Dierks Bentley, and The B52s — with $5.88M in 2025 historical revenue, projecting $6M+ in value on-chain for the 2026 series, and individual ticket prices ranging from $400 to $8,000. Sellout currently operates nationwide with over 200,000 registered users and over 250,000 tickets issued. This is the kind of mission-critical commercial workload aligned with the Cardano 2030 Vision, where Cardano's eUTxO determinism, CIP-68 metadata, and native multi-asset architecture uniquely fit to solve real-world problems.

Real businesses, real revenue, building products for the real world, providing real value back to the Treasury.

## 4. Funding & Treasury Repayment Structure

This is a treasury withdrawal of ₳4,969,231 (~$1,093,231 USD at $0.22/ADA) over 8 months, disbursed against milestones (see §6). Beyond standard milestone gating, this proposal returns value to the Treasury through two commitments.

### Contingency return

Conditionally, we agree to return our contingency of approximately 3% (136,363 ADA) to the Treasury if it remains unused upon project completion.

### Revenue-share repayment to cost recovery

Beyond the contingency, this proposal commits to a defined share of marketplace and royalty revenue paid back to the Cardano Treasury, sized and timelined to recover the Treasury's contribution (~$1.09M USD).

**What's set in this proposal vs. set through contracting.** The structural commitment to repay is fixed in this proposal. The exact share percentage, the precise revenue base definition, and the repayment cadence are established through M1 contracting with the appointed oversight committee. These specifics require commercial discussion that cannot be pre-negotiated unilaterally in the proposal text. Repayment progress is reported publicly each quarter from launch onward, so cost-recovery progress is transparent against a defined denominator from day one.

**The structure:**

- The share applies to a defined revenue base — the platform's marketplace fee revenue and the share of royalty revenue attributable to the new operating infrastructure. Definitions and reporting cadence are finalized in M1 contracting with the oversight committee.
- The share is paid back to the Cardano Treasury (or a designated treasury-controlled receiving vehicle) on a quarterly cadence.
- A percentage of all fees (25%) is sent to the Treasury until the Treasury is reimbursed in full.
- The share continues until the cumulative repayment reaches the Treasury's USD contribution to this withdrawal. The exact percentage and timeline are finalized in M1 contracting; the structural commitment to repay is fixed here.
- Once the Treasury is paid back, this will be reduced to a smaller, permanent fee (5%).
- Repayment progress is reported publicly each quarter, with full revenue-base and share-calculation transparency to the oversight committee.

The intent is straightforward: the Treasury funds the build, real commercial activity runs through Cardano, and a defined portion of the revenue that activity produces is returned to the Treasury until the funding is recovered.

### Why this approach, not equity or a token

The previous draft of this proposal contemplated an equity grant in a new operating entity. After community feedback, we have pulled that mechanism in favor of the structure above, for four reasons:

- **Treasury governance fit.** A direct rev-share back to the Treasury is a cleaner instrument for a treasury-funded proposal than a private-company equity stake. The Cardano Treasury is a community-governed treasury, not a simple venture vehicle; equity in a private US LLC creates custody, valuation, dilution, and governance-rights questions that do not arise with a quarterly USD repayment stream.
- **No token.** No tradable token is issued. The instrument is a contractual repayment obligation on operating revenue, not an asset that can be speculated on or that creates regulatory complexity.
- **Aligned with how Cardano already earns from this work.** Protocol fees already flow back to the network from every on-chain action. The revenue-share mechanism layers on top of that natural flow — same direction, same alignment — until the explicit USD contribution is repaid.
- **Skin in the game is structural, not promised.** The repayment is enforceable through the M1 contracting framework with the appointed oversight committee. It is a defined contractual obligation, not a future commitment that depends on memory or goodwill.

### Items to be finalized through contracting

- The exact revenue base (whether the share applies to gross marketplace fees, net of payment processing, etc.).
- The receiving entity on the Treasury side — a direct Treasury wallet or a designated treasury-controlled receiving vehicle — is a question for the oversight committee.
- The reporting and audit cadence (quarterly with annual external audit is our proposed starting point).
- The on-chain attestation mechanism for the contractual repayment obligation, likely a CIP-100 governance metadata anchor of the executed agreement.

### Treasury protections

- **Milestone-gated disbursement** — funds are released only upon verified delivery. No delivery, no payment.
- **Contingency return** — the ~3% contingency (136,363 ADA) is returned to the Treasury if unused.
- **Undisbursed-fund protection** — if any milestone is not delivered within its window, the corresponding disbursement is held; undisbursed funds remain under Treasury control.
- **Independent verification** — an independent third-party smart-contract audit, oversight committee review, and public quarterly reporting.
- **Revenue-share repayment** — a defined contractual obligation returning revenue to the Treasury until cost recovery.

### What this is not

- **Not a condition of milestone disbursement.** The rev-share is a standalone commercial obligation finalized in M1 contracting; the work package's milestone gating, oversight review, and refund conditions remain exactly as specified.
- **Not a token offering.** No tradable token is issued.
- **Not a substitute for any deliverable.** Every deliverable in M1–M5 is independently funded and gated. The repayment is additive value, on top of the work being done.

## 7. Risk Mitigation

**Milestone gating.** Funds are tied to defined milestone deliverables, oversight committee review, an independent third-party smart-contract audit, and public quarterly reporting. Every disbursement is traceable to a verified deliverable. No delivery, no payment. Milestone gating prevents cascading risk: if any single workstream slips, the corresponding disbursement is held until deliverable acceptance.

**Co-investment.** Sellout funded Phase 1 from its own capital (over $130k invested). The Treasury is not the only capital at stake — the anchor business and its anchor deployment are already committed.

**Course correction.** Quarterly reporting is a working tool, not a documentation exercise. If a metric is off-track, we will identify the gap, diagnose the cause, and present recommended adjustments to the oversight committee.

**Partnership continuity / key-person risk.** The proposal depends on the Anvil–Sellout working relationship. To bound the risk to the Treasury:

- Anvil is the sole contracting/responsible party for this withdrawal. All disbursements are gated on Anvil deliverables; Sellout participates as the operating client and anchor deployment under that contract.
- The Yellowstone Club anchor deployment is contracted at the Sellout-venue layer; the proposal's commercial activation depends on it, but the on-chain infrastructure outlasts any single anchor deployment and is reusable by other operators.

**Post-funding sustainability.** After Phase 2 ships, the on-chain infrastructure is maintained by Sellout as part of normal operating costs. Sellout's revenue model of service fees on primary and secondary ticket sales generates the ongoing operating budget for the platform. No further treasury withdrawal is planned or anticipated. If a future Phase 3 expansion proposal makes sense due to rapid success (additional verticals, additional integrations), it would be a separate, scoped proposal and not a dependency of this one.
